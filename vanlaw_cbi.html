Guide to disclosure of confidential business information under the Vanessa’s Law authority

Quick Links

  • Google
  • Google2

Health Canada is increasing the amount of information it publishes on regulatory decisions and inspection, compliance and enforcement activities regarding drugs. This information is publicly available on Health Canada web sites and databases.

Confidential business information (CBI) may be included in a variety of regulatory documents that Health Canada requires drug companies to provide regarding the safety, efficacy and quality of their products. In certain situations, where publicly available information is not sufficient, access to information that may contain CBI can assist the work of health researchers and practitioners, contributing to improved patient safety and better health outcomes for Canadians.

Under the Food and Drugs Act (paragraph 21.1(3)(c)), the Minister of Health has discretionary authority to disclose confidential business information to an individual or corporation for the purpose of protecting or promoting human health or the safety of the public. This discretionary authority was added to the Food and Drugs Act by the Protecting Canadians from Unsafe Drugs Act (Vanessa’s Law) and came into force in November 2014. It complements other changes introduced to strengthen Health Canada’s capacity to respond quickly to health risks and to promote greater confidence in the oversight of therapeutic products by increasing transparency. These changes are summarized in a guide to the new authorities introduced in Vanessa’s Law.

To whom can confidential business information be disclosed?

An individual or corporation that carries out functions relating to the protection or promotion human health or the safety of the public may request the disclosure of information that contains confidential business information. Health Canada will consider requests only from individuals with relevant professional qualifications, and from corporations with a legitimate mandate relating to human health or public safety. Health Canada will consider requests only when the purpose of the disclosure is related to the protection or promotion of human health or the safety of the public.

Health Canada will not disclose information to individuals or organizations deemed to have a conflict of interest, or where the confidential business information would be used for commercial purposes.

What information can be disclosed?

The Minister of Health’s discretionary authority to disclose confidential business information applies to information related to therapeutic products, including prescription and non-prescription drugs, vaccines, blood and blood products, donor semen for assisted conception, gene and cell therapies, tissues and organs, and medical devices. It does not apply to natural health products.

Disclosure of confidential business information will only be considered where it relates to safety and efficacy information used to support a therapeutic product already authorized by Health Canada for marketing in Canada. Information currently under review or submitted to Health Canada in support of a product that has not yet been authorized by Health Canada will not be disclosed. Health Canada will not disclose interim results of ongoing clinical trials if disclosure, where disclosure would jeapordize the outcome of the study, unless there is a significant health reason for doing so.

How to request the disclosure of confidential business information?

Complete the request form. Be sure to provide:

  1. The relevant qualifications of all individuals (including all collaborators) who will require access to disclosed information containing CBI. For requests from corporations, provide the mandate and how the corporation carries out functions related to the protection or promotion of human health or public safety.
  2. A project proposal that clearly indicates the purpose of the disclosure and how it relates to the protection or promotion of human health or the safety of the Canadian public.

To assist you in identifying types of regulatory information (Section 2 of the Request Form), please refer to the “Tools for finding regulatory information”.

How does Health Canada decide whether or not to disclose confidential business information?

Health Canada's decision is based on rigorous scientific evaluation and a process that ensures fair and consistent review of requests. Key steps on the process are as follows:

  1. Screening: Requests are reviewed to ensure that they are complete, including proof of qualifications, corporate mandate, and a project description that includes a specific purpose for seeking access to CBI. Incomplete requests will be returned to the applicant.
  2. Assessment: Complete requests are referred to a Review Committee for assessment. The Committee will assess the requestor’s qualifications and corporate mandate to determine whether they carry out functions relating to the protection or promotion of human health or the safety of the public. The Committee will also review the proposed project to determine whether it relates to the protection or promotion of human health or the safety of the public. A request is denied if it does not meet these requirements.
  3. Record search: For a request that meets the requirements of the assessment, Health Canada will conduct a search of its records. If the records contain no CBI, the requestor will be notified and provided an opportunity to obtain the information from the appropriate Health Canada office/ATIP division.
  4. Communication with requestor: If CBI is included in records relating to an assessed request, the requestor will be notified and, where large volumes of records have been identified, may be consulted to refine their request. At this stage the requestor will be asked to complete a Conflict of Interest Declaration and to sign a Confidentiality Agreement. The signing of a Confidentiality Agreement by the requestor at this point in the process does not mean that information will be disclosed. Further information may also be required in order to complete Health Canada’s review of the request.
  5. Recommendation: Once the requestor has provided a completed Conflict of Interest Declaration, signed Confidentiality Agreement and any other required information, the Review Committee will prepare a recommendation on what records, if any, should be disclosed. Only information that is directly related to the purpose of the project will be considered in the recommendation.
  6. Decision: The Review Committee recommendation is considered by a senior Health Canada official designated to exercise the Minister’s authority to disclose CBI under the Food and Drugs Act (section 21.1(3)(c)). This official will make the decision and if any information will be disclosed, the official will sign the Confidentiality Agreement. The Disclosure of information requires that the requester attest to having proper information storage and handling safeguards in place.
  7. Notification to requestor: Health Canada will notify the requestor of its decision. When the decision is to disclose CBI, arrangements will be made to ensure secure transmission.
  8. Notification to originator: Health Canada will notify the originator following transmission of the information to an applicant.
  9. Notification of the public: Information regarding the request, including a description of the project and the terms and conditions of the confidentiality agreement, may be made public.

Results generated from proposed project

Any safety signals generated from the project undertaken with disclosed information are expected to be shared with Health Canada. The relevant regulatory programs will assess evidence generated by projects and Health Canada will not hesitate to take any necessary action to safeguard the health and safety of Canadians.

Will there be a confidentiality agreement in place between Health Canada and the requestor?

Yes. Before disclosing the information, Health Canada requires requestors to sign a legally binding Confidentiality Agreement with stringent requirements to maintain the confidentiality of disclosed information. Among other things, this Agreement provides that the disclosed information can be used only for the purposes of the approved project and must be kept confidential using appropriate safeguards. The Confidentiality Agreement also prohibits reproduction of disclosed information. The researcher is free to publish results of the analysis as long as the confidentiality of the disclosed information is maintained.

Additional information for requestors

There are other authorities in the Food and Drugs Act that Health Canada to disclose CBI when there is a risk of serious injury to human health, and for Health Canada to disclose CBI to other government agencies, including regulatory counterparts and members of advisory bodies.

  • Serious risk of injury to human health (section 21.1(2))
  • To a government (section 21.1(3)(a)
  • To a person from whom the Minister seeks advice (section 21.1(3)(b))

For more information on the implementation of the Protecting Canadians from Unsafe Drugs Act (Vanessa’s Law) - (in force November 6, 2014) please refer to the following guide:
