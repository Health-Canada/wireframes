The Drug and Health Product Register provides Canadians with important information about drug products. Search consumer information on drug product use, safety, common and reported side effects.

Overview

The DHPR gives Canadians easy access to consumer information about:

  • drug product use,
  • drug product safety, and
  • common and reported drug product side effects.

You can use the DHPR to search for information about drug products. You can search by:

  • brand name,
  • active ingredient, and
  • drug identification number (DIN).

Some information available through the DHPR includes:

  • what a medication is used for,
  • what it does,
  • when it should not be used,
  • what the medicinal ingredients are,
  • what the non-medicinal ingredients are, and
  • warnings and precautions.

Other DHPR information includes:

  • what dosage form the medication comes in,
  • interactions with this medication,
  • proper use of this medication,
  • how to store the medication,
  • side effects and what to do about them, and
  • how to report side effects.

All information available through the DHPR has been approved by Health Canada.

Why you might be interested

You may be interested in the DHPR if you:

  • care about the safety of the health products you are using,
  • want to know how to use health products effectively, and
  • want access to health product regulatory information and data.

While the DHPR is designed for all Canadians, it may be especially useful to:

  • consumers,
  • health care providers,
  • professionals delivering health care services, and
  • researchers interested in Canada's health care system.

Our commitment

Health Canada's Health Products and Food Branch (HPFB) regulates health products and provides information on their safety and proper use. We are a trusted source of information about drug and health products.

However, we recognize that it can be difficult to find information on our website . So we are improving access to health product information by presenting it in a searchable, user-friendly format. This conforms with the federal Open Government initiative that is making more information available to Canadians. It will also help Canadians make better-informed decisions about their health.

We believe that:

  • you should be able to search public information based on what your interests are,
  • you should be able to view information in a format that is easy to understand, and
  • you should be able to find all the relevant information in one place.

Top of page

How the DHPR is delivering on these commitments

We have designed the DHPR as a virtual warehouse. It will collect and store the information we gather during our regulatory activities. It will also deliver precise and meaningful information that explains health product safety.

Currently the DHPR provides the top 100 prescribed products (innovator and generic). They are selected based on their top 2013 Canadian sales (IMS provided statistics).

The DHPR also has bilingual consumer information sections.

An additional 250 products are included that consist largely of generic and some innovator products. These have the same active ingredient(s) as the Top 100.

The first release of the DHPR in winter 2015 will enable Canadians to test the system. We are asking for ongoing feedback on how easy and fast it is to search for the information you want. This feedback will help Health Canada improve the DHPR for future releases.

Additionally, future expansions of DHPR content could include regulated medical devices and natural health products. Future DHPR content could also include other key federal government initiatives like the transparency and openness initiatives.

Ultimately, the DHPR will be the authoritative source of publically available information about health product safety and use. This will be based on Health Canada's regulatory activities.
