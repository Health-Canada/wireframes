Regulatory Decision Summary: BIOPLEX 2200 SYSTEM

Device Class:Class IV

What was the application for at the time of approval?

The application is for a new Class IV in vitro diagnostic medical device. The BioPlex 2200 HIV Ag-Ab assay is a multiflow immunoassay, intended for laboratory use and based on the principle of the sandwich technique for the detection of Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) antigen and the various antibodies associated with HIV-1 and HIV-2 virus in human serum or plasma.

The BioPlex 2200 HIV Ag-Ab assay is a multiplex flow immunoassay intended for the simultaneous qualitative detection and differentiation of the individual analytes HIV-1 p24 antigen, HIV-1 (groups M and O) antibodies, and HIV-2 antibodies in human serum or plasma (fresh or frozen K2 EDTA, K3 EDTA, lithium heparin, sodium heparin; fresh citrate). It can be used as a combination test that reports a single result for all the HIV analytes or as an individual test for any of the specific HIV analytes. This kit is intended as an aid in the diagnosis of infection with HIV-1 and/or HIV-2, including acute or primary HIV-1 infection. The assay may also be used as an aid in the diagnosis of infection with HIV-1 and/or HIV-2 in pediatric subjects as young as two years of age, and pregnant women.

The BioPlex 2200 HIV Ag-Ab assay is also intended for use in testing serum and plasma specimens to screen donors whose cells, tissues and organs are intended for transplant while the donor"s heart is still beating. This assay is not indicated for use with specimens from cadaveric (non-heart-beating) donors.

The BioPlex 2200 HIV Ag-Ab assay is intended for use with the BioPlex 2200 System.

What information did Health Canada review?

Safety and effectiveness was supported by a Declaration of Conformity to applicable Health Canada recognized standards. Analytical performance claims for HIV-1 p24 Ag sensitivity, HIV Antigen subtype detection, antigen and antibody HIV subtypes sensitivity, dual infection detection, analytical specificity, including HIV cross-reactivity, interference with endogenous and exogenous substances, hook effect, reproducibility, precision, sample type and cut-off validation were demonstrated. Intended software was verified and validated. All stability claims for samples and reagents were properly substantiated. Manufacturing process including quality processes were validated.

Clinical studies were designed to meet the performance requirements set out in Health Canada's Guidance document and the European Common Technical Specifications for HIV laboratory assays. Over 8000 blood donor and low risk population samples were tested to support an overall clinical specificity claim of greater than 99.8%. One thousand nine hundred and twelve (1912) confirmed HIV-1 and 200 HIV-2 positive samples were tested to demonstrate a 100% sensitivity. The studies provided support the claims of the use of the assay as an aid in the diagnosis of infection with HIV-1 and /or HIV-2, including primary or acute HIV infection and the use of the assay in paediatric subjects and pregnant women.

Labelling, including instructions for use, meet the requirements of the Medical Device Regulations.

Decision issued

Approved; issued licence in accordance with Section 36(1)(1) of the  Medical Devices Regulations.

Date of decision

2015-06-25
